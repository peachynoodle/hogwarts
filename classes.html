Classes

Required Courses

All first-years must take the following classes. These will be taken through the student's fifth year at Hogwarts.

Transfiguration

Transfiguration is the magic when changing the form and appearance of an object. It is possible to turn inanimate objects into animate ones and vice versa. Some Transfiguration spells can alter parts of an object. Learning to become an Animagus is also possible, but is a difficult higher magic. The class is taught by Professor Minerva McGonagall.
Textbooks: A Beginner's Guide to Transfiguration, A Intermediate Transfiguration, Guide to Advanced Transfiguration, and Theories of Transubstantial Transfiguration

Charms

The charms class involves trying and practicing various charms that are helpful for everyday wizarding life. Some of these include the Levitation, Cheering, Summoning, Banishing, Silencing, and Colour Change Charms. The class is taught by Professor Flitwick.
Textbooks: Achievements in Charming, An Anthology of Eighteenth Century Charms, Olde and Forgotten Bewitchments and Charmes, and Quintessence: A Quest

Potions

The charms class involves memorizing properties of a variety of potion ingredients, the composition of specific potions, and practicing their preparation. There will also be essays on the properties of potion ingredients. Some of the potions taught include: the Boil Cure Potion, Forgetfulness Potion, Shrinking Solution, and antidotes. The class is taught by Professor Snape.
Textbooks: Encyclopedia of Potions, Magical and Non-Magical Plants, and Potions
Equipment: Cauldron, Knife, Mortar and Pestle, Potion-Making Kit, Brass Scales

Herbology

The herbology class involves the study of magical plants and fungi. This includes how to care for them and their magical properties and uses. Some are used as potion ingredients, and others have magical effects on their own. Classes take place in the greenhouses and include many hands-on-activities; this includes pruning, repotting, and harvesting. The class is taught by Professor Sprout.
Textbooks: One Thousand Magical Herbs and Fungi and Encyclopedia of Magical and Mundane Plants
Equipment: Dragon Dung Compost, Dragon-hide Gloves, Eat Muffs, Slug Pellets, Flesh-Eating Slug Repellent, Mooncalf Dung

Defence Against the Dark Arts

This class focuses on teaching students defensive magic against the Dark Arts so they will be able to protect themselves in times of need. It is often referred to as DADA class. Students earn experience dealing with boggarts and other Dark Creatures. They will also duel others, under supervision, with self-defense spells such as the Disarming Charm. Students will also be taught the Patronus Charm, an incredibly important spell for fighting off Dementors. The class is taught by Professor Lockhart.
Textbooks: Curses and Counter-Curses, and Dark Forces: A Guide to Self-Protection

Astronomy

The astronomy class involves the study of stars and constellations, planets and their moons, and the motions of these. Students will also learn how to use teloscopes to observe the night sky from. Students in their first five years are required to take the class. It takes place in the Astronomy Tower at midnight. Students will be able to fill in a star chart by the end of the year. The class is taught by Professor Sinistra.
Textbooks: Will be provided in class.
Equipment: Teloscopes, Star Charts, Reference Books

History of Magic

This class focuses on various aspects of magical history. It is a lecture based class and goes through the beginning of magical history to present and touches upon everything from goblin riots to giant wars. The class is taught by Professor Binns.
Textbook: A History of Magic

Flying Lessons (Compulsory)

The Flying Lessons class is self-explanitory. Students will learn how to fly on a broom, and those who catch on quickly will be recommended to join either the Broom Club or their House Quidditch Team. The class is taught by Professor Hooch.
Equipment: Brooms will be provided in class.

Electives

These are chosen at the end of a student's second year. There is a minimum requirement of 2 electives, but a student may take up to 3. These classes will be taken from a student's third to fifth year.

Arithmancy

This class focuses on magic and magical theory. Students are expected to write thorough essays and understand complicated number charts. The class is taught by Professor Vector.
Textbooks: Numerology and Gramatica, and New Theory of Numerology

Muggle Studies

Muggle Studies focuses on the history, culture, and psychology of non-magical people. It attempts to aid young witches and wizards in understanding the difference of mindsets between Muggles and wizards, while emphasizing that the two are not as different as many perceive them to be. The class is taught by Professor Burbage.
Textbooks: Home Life and Social Habits of British Muggles
Equipment: Books, Parchment, Quill

Divination

This class focuses on the magic that attempts to see into the future. Students will learn about the different types of Divination; "fortunetelling", centaur-divination, Seeing, and True Seeing.

Care of Magical Creatures

This class teaches about the behaviors, properties, habitats, and care of magical creatures. Hands-On interaction with the creatures themselves is to be expected, for one has to see the real thing up close and personal to truly understand how these creatures really act. The class mainly takes place outdoors, and it is taught by Rubeus Hagrid.
Textbooks: Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them, and Monster Book of Monsters
